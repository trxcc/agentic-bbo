# Domain Prior Knowledge

- Placement objectives such as HPWL typically respond to spatial structure; smooth local search can help once a feasible region is found, but multimodality and constraint interaction are common.
- Coordinates are continuous on a fixed grid extent; extremely tight clustering of macros may interact badly with routing or legalization behavior in the underlying tool, depending on benchmark and placer.
- Because feedback is slow and constraints are strong, exploration strategies that preserve diversity early and respect bounds strictly tend to be more robust than aggressive exploitation from a single seed.
